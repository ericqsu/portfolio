As mentioned previously, the Battle of the Coral Sea was the closest battle to mainland Australia, as well as the first aircraft carrier v aircraft carrier battle. This exhibit will show what occured during the battle., in which would in tern help us understand its intensity.











Dels journey - Map of the battle




First and second day (4-5th May)




Both American carriers were targeted during the afternoons by dive bombers, their location being exposed by Japanese spy planes throughout the morning of each day. The first two days of the battle was nearly casualty free. The battle was in favour of the allies thus far after the Japanese carrier Shoho was heavily damaged and sunk later on the 7th.






Third, fourth and last day (6-8th May)




Japanese targeting kept continuing, having multiple bombs dropped by the Japanese. On the 8th, the carrier USS Lexington sunk and the Japanese retreated also after heavy losses. totally with 656 casualties for the allies and 966 for the Japanese.
